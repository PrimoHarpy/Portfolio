Project 1: Regenerate

Create a new narrative approach to old essays.

Project Details

  • Year: 2022

  • Type: Publication

Project 3: Glass Cannon Volver

Exaggerate the branding tactics vapes use to target kids.

Project Details

  • Client: University 3rd year Project
  • Year: 2024
  • Tools: Indesign, Photoshop, Procreate
  • Type: Provocative Design
This project was inspired when I visited a vape shop and noticed all the colors, names, and the overall aesthetic. It made me question why it seemed so geared toward kids, even though it wasn't intended for them. Then I realized that a close relative of vapes had done this in the past‚Äîsubtle branding aimed at kids, even when it was illegal for them to consume it. I'm talking about tobacco companies. So, I started researching and came up with the idea of creating my own branding aimed at kids, but in such an exaggerated way that it would also grab the attention of adults and make them realize how absurd the marketing tactics of vapes are.
So, I started producing some content about tobacco with a satirical tone. Then, I came across a vape that could change flavors with the motion of a revolver. That‚Äôs when the idea hit me: What if I made a vape revolver? Interestingly, you have to aim it at your mouth to smoke. This perfectly blended with the outcome I wanted. It would attract kids because it looks like a toy gun, but if an adult saw it, they‚Äôd definitely question why a kid is aiming a gun at their mouth‚Äîeven if it‚Äôs just a toy. So, I created a prototype of the vape, making it functional, even though it‚Äôs just a prototype.

Project 5: Shatered Hiragani

Create a new typeface

Project Details

  • Client: University 1st year Project
  • Year: 2021
  • Tools: Indesign
  • Type: Typeface
For this project, the professors gave us a random theme to create a typeface that reflected it. My theme was 'Shattered.' After brainstorming, the image that kept popping up was plates shattering. So, I decided to create a font and place it on a plate, then break the plate to capture the type from the shards. The next step was selecting the base type for the project. I created my own stylish base type and added a straight line to serve as a reference in case the shattering affected legibility. I ended up creating multiple versions of each letter by taking photos of the fragments in different positions. From that process, I developed two typefaces: one stylish and legible, and one with the least legible version, based on the pictures I took of the shards.

Project 8: Vertere

Create an ethical Brand

Project Details

  • Client: University 2nd year Project
  • Year: 2022
  • Tools: Indesign, Photoshop, Sewing Machine
  • Type: Brand Developing
For this project, I drew inspiration from a presentation we had about the environment and how to design ethically. One fact that stood out was the highest recorded temperature (from 2022), so I decided to base the project around that number. It was in Sicily, and the temperature reached 48.8 degrees.
I decided to create a fashion brand, but instead of focusing on building a strong brand identity, I wanted to emphasize the issue itself. The idea was that the design style and the concept of the brand would make a name for itself, rather than relying on a specific name or logo. I aimed to create different drop concepts, such as featuring the highest temperature recorded that year, the location, and centering the drop around that place and temperature. I designed a conceptual T-shirt, some tags, and a small publication that would accompany each purchase. The booklet included visual responses and concepts exploring how global warming would impact Sicily, in this case. I also had a video interview with a Sicilian, but unfortunately, I lost the footage. My goal was to make the issue more personal‚Äîseeing who and what is affected‚Äîbringing the problem closer to home rather than keeping it distant.
